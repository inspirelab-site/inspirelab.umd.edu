The interaction between random and systematic visual stimulation and infraslow quasi-periodic spatiotemporal patterns of whole-brain activity
Nan Xu, Derek M. Smith, George Jeno, Dolly T. Seeburger, Eric H. Schumacher, Shella D. Keilholz
Imaging Neuroscience  ·  01 Aug 2023  · doi:10.1162/imag_a_00002
Employed the statistical inferences techniques to study the interplay between infraslow dynamic processes and visual perceptions. Discovered that these intrinsic dynamic processes are notably resilient in humans, as contrast with anesthetized rodents (Belloy et al., 2021). Yet, their stability can still be affected in diverse ways: they can swamp an overlapping BOLD response evoked by the flickering checkerboard, but their phase-locked waveform can still be modulated by different sequences of stimuli that affects the reaction time and sustained attention.

Reconstruction of Stochastic 3D Signals With Symmetric Statistics From 2D Projection Images Motivated by Cryo-Electron Microscopy
Nan Xu, Peter C. Doerschuk
IEEE Transactions on Image Processing  ·  01 Nov 2019  · doi:10.1109/tip.2019.2915631
Using cryo-EM, we transform highly noisy and randomly-oriented projection images of 103─106 virus particles into a 3D model of the virus. Unlike traditional methods that assume strict geometric symmetry, we adopted a more realistic approach, allowing individual particles to be asymmetric while maintaining symmetry in the overall mean and covariance. This novel method eliminates long-standing distortions along the symmetry axes.
